title: Contract Marketplace

The Contract Marketplace is the primary view for discovering and reserving energy contracts. It displays all available contracts in a two-column card grid alongside a persistent filter panel.

## Layout

The page uses a four-column layout: the leftmost column contains the Filters panel, and the remaining three columns display the contract card grid. A sort control and an "Available" contract count appear in the top-right header area.

## Filtering Contracts

The **Filters** panel on the left lets you narrow results across five dimensions. All filters apply immediately — the contract list re-fetches from the API whenever any filter value changes.

### Energy Type

Eight energy type toggle buttons are available:

<CardGroup cols={4}>
  <Card title="Solar" />
  <Card title="Wind" />
  <Card title="Nuclear" />

Click any button to activate it (highlighted in blue). Click again to deactivate. Multiple energy types can be selected simultaneously — the filter returns contracts matching any of the selected types.

### Price Range

Two numeric inputs — **Min Price** and **Max Price** — accept values in $/MWh. The default range is $0–$100/MWh.

### Location

A text input filters contracts by delivery location. The field accepts partial text (e.g. `Texas`) and sends it as a `location` query parameter to the API.

### Quantity Range

Min and max quantity fields (in MWh) are available via the `filters` state (`min_quantity`, `max_quantity`). These are reset alongside the other filters when **Reset** is clicked.

### Date Range

Start and end date inputs (`start_date`, `end_date`) filter contracts by their delivery period. These are also part of the `filters` state managed alongside the quantity fields.

### Resetting Filters

Click the **Reset** link at the top of the Filters panel to restore all filter values to their defaults:

- Energy types: none selected
- Min price: 0, Max price: 100
- Location: empty
- Min/max quantity: empty
- Start/end date: empty
- Sort: `delivery_start` ascending

## Sorting

The sort control appears in the top-right header. Use the dropdown to choose a sort field:

| Option | Sort Field |
|--------|------------|
| Date | `delivery_start` |
| Price | `price_per_mwh` |
| Quantity | `quantity_mwh` |

Click the **ASC / DESC** toggle button next to the dropdown to flip the sort direction.

## Contract Cards

Each contract is displayed as a card showing:

- **Energy type** and **location**
- **Status badge** — green for `Available`, red for any other status
- **Quantity** in MWh
- **Price** in $/MWh
- A **Details** button and an **Add** button

### Viewing Contract Details

Click the **Details** button (Info icon) on any card to open the Contract Detail modal. The modal shows:

- Energy type and current status
- Provider information
- Delivery location
- Carbon intensity (kg CO2/MWh)
- Delivery period (start and end dates)
- Pricing structure (fixed price per MWh)
- Total capacity (MWh)
- Contract description

A **Reserve Contract Now** button at the bottom of the modal adds the contract to your portfolio and closes the modal. The button is disabled if the contract is not `Available` or if it is already in your portfolio.

### Adding a Contract to Your Portfolio

<Steps>
  <Step title="Locate the contract card">
    Browse or filter the marketplace to find the contract you want.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add">
    Click the **Add** button on the contract card. The button label changes to **Added** once the contract is in your portfolio.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm in Portfolio">
    Navigate to **My Portfolio** in the sidebar to verify the contract appears there.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The **Add** button is disabled when the contract status is not `Available` or when the contract is already in your portfolio.
</Note>

## Comparing Contracts

You can select up to 3 contracts for side-by-side comparison.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click the compare icon">
    Click the **Scale** icon button in the top-right corner of any contract card. The icon turns into a blue checkmark when the contract is selected for comparison.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select additional contracts">
    Repeat for one or two more contracts. The comparison toolbar appears at the bottom of the screen showing the selected contracts' initials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the comparison modal">
    Click **Compare N Selected** in the floating toolbar. This button is disabled until at least 2 contracts are selected. The comparison modal opens showing the contracts side by side.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  A maximum of 3 contracts can be added to the comparison list at once. Selecting a fourth contract has no effect.
</Warning>

The floating comparison toolbar is only visible when at least one contract has been selected for comparison.
